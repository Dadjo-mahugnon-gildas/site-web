M. Gildas Dadjo, Ph.D candidate in Applied Mathematics

I started my PhD degree in September 2022. My work aims to develop control methods to pilot an original system of three interconnected bioreactors to reuse wastewater in agriculture. More particularly, it is a problem called "setpoint tracking", such "setpoint" being the result of the resolution of an optimal control problem solved using a crop irrigation model. However, optimal solutions should be expressed as feedback that depends on the nitrogen in the soil, which a priori is not available. Another difficulty is related to the various uncertainties in the model parameters. We propose to bring concrete answers to these challenges by designing the so-called "adaptive observer" for the joint estimation of nitrogen and uncertain parameters.
